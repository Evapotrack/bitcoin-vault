BITCOIN VAULT — RECOVERY AND BACKUP GUIDE
A non-technical, step-by-step guide for restoring your vault
Creator: Andrew Brown
Date: April 6, 2026
================================================================================

WHO THIS DOCUMENT IS FOR

This document is written for someone who needs to restore a Bitcoin Vault from
a backup. That person might be you on a new computer, or it might be someone
you've trusted with your seed phrase — a family member, an executor, or an
inheritor. It assumes no prior experience with Bitcoin or encryption software.

Keep this document with your seed backup or give a copy to the person who
would need it.


================================================================================
WHAT YOU NEED TO RESTORE YOUR VAULT
================================================================================

You need exactly two things:

    1. YOUR 12-WORD SEED PHRASE
       Twelve English words in a specific order. This is the master key to
       everything — your Bitcoin and your encrypted files. It was written
       down on paper or stamped into metal during the initial setup of the
       vault. It looks something like:

           1. abandon   2. ability   3. able     4. about
           5. above     6. absent    7. absorb   8. abstract
           9. absurd   10. abuse    11. access   12. accident

       (These are example words, not a real seed. Your words will be different.)

       The ORDER matters. Word 1 must be word 1. If you swap any two words,
       the restore will fail.

    2. YOUR ENCRYPTED VAULT FOLDER
       A folder on a backup drive, iCloud, or Time Machine that contains
       your encrypted files. The default folder name is "BitcoinVault" and
       it was originally located in the Documents folder on the Mac where
       the vault was set up. Inside, you will see files with random-looking
       names ending in .enc — these are your encrypted files. They are
       unreadable without the seed phrase.

       If you have access to an iCloud account, a Time Machine backup, or
       an external drive that was used for backups, look for this folder.


WHAT HAPPENS WITH EACH PIECE ALONE:

    Seed phrase + encrypted folder = FULL RECOVERY (files + Bitcoin)
    Seed phrase alone             = BITCOIN ONLY (wallet balance recoverable,
                                    but files cannot be decrypted without
                                    the encrypted folder)
    Encrypted folder alone        = NOTHING (files are encrypted and cannot
                                    be read without the seed phrase)

If you have only the seed phrase, you can still recover any Bitcoin that was
in the vault's wallet. But the files are gone without the encrypted folder.

If you have only the encrypted folder, the files are permanently locked.
There is no backdoor. There is no support team. There is no reset.


================================================================================
STEP-BY-STEP RECOVERY
================================================================================

STEP 1: GET A MAC

The Bitcoin Vault app runs on macOS. You need a Mac — any recent Mac running
macOS Sequoia or later should work. If the original Mac was lost or destroyed,
any replacement Mac will do.


STEP 2: INSTALL THE APP

Download the Bitcoin Vault app from:

    github.com/Evapotrack/bitcoin-vault

Look for the "Releases" section on the right side of the page. Download the
latest .dmg file. Open it and drag the app to your Applications folder, just
like any other Mac app.

If the Mac says the app is from an "unidentified developer," go to System
Settings > Privacy & Security and click "Open Anyway."


STEP 3: LAUNCH AND CHOOSE RESTORE

When you open the app for the first time, you will see a welcome screen with
two options:

    "Create New Vault"           — Do NOT click this.
    "Restore Existing Vault"     — Click this.


STEP 4: ENTER YOUR 12 WORDS

The app will show you 12 numbered text fields. Type each word from your seed
phrase into the correct numbered field. Make sure:

    - Every word is spelled correctly
    - Every word is in the correct position (word 1 in slot 1, etc.)
    - All words are lowercase
    - There are no extra spaces before or after any word

If you make a mistake, the app will tell you the seed phrase is invalid.
Double-check your words and try again. The most common errors are:

    - Swapped words (word 3 and word 4 in the wrong positions)
    - Misspelled words (the words come from a specific list of 2,048 words —
      "abscent" is not valid, "absent" is)
    - Missing a word (entering only 11 words)


STEP 5: POINT TO YOUR ENCRYPTED FOLDER

The app will ask you to select the folder containing your backed-up encrypted
vault files. Navigate to wherever your backup is:

    - If on an external drive: plug it in, open Finder, navigate to the drive
    - If on iCloud: open Finder, look in the iCloud Drive section
    - If on Time Machine: open Time Machine, navigate to the date when the
      vault was last known to be working, find the folder

Select the folder that contains the .enc files. It might be called
"BitcoinVault" or whatever name was chosen during original setup.


STEP 6: WAIT FOR RESTORATION

The app will:

    1. Derive encryption keys from your seed phrase
    2. Attempt to decrypt the vault index (the encrypted map of your files)
    3. Scan the Bitcoin blockchain for any funds in your wallet

This may take a minute or two. If successful, you will see a message:

    "Vault restored."

If the app says "Could not decrypt vault with this seed," then either:
    - The seed words are wrong or in the wrong order — go back and recheck
    - The encrypted folder is from a different vault (different seed)
    - The encrypted folder is corrupted

If the app says "Wallet restored. No encrypted vault files found," then:
    - The seed is correct (your Bitcoin is accessible)
    - But the folder you selected does not contain vault files
    - Look for a different backup location


STEP 7: VERIFY YOUR FILES

Once restored, the app will show the lock screen. You will need to send a
Bitcoin payment to unlock the vault and access your files (the same process
as normal use).

If you are an inheritor and the dead man's switch has been activated (the
original owner has not made a proof-of-life payment within the set time
period), the vault may open without a Bitcoin payment — just the seed.

After unlocking, browse through the files and folders to confirm everything
is present and accessible.


STEP 8: SECURE THE NEW INSTALLATION

Once you have confirmed the restore is successful:

    - Set up a new backup of the vault folder on this Mac (iCloud, Time
      Machine, or external drive)
    - Store the seed phrase securely again (do not leave it sitting out)
    - Consider whether the vault's unlock cost and frequency settings need
      to be changed for your use


================================================================================
HOW TO BACK UP YOUR VAULT (FOR CURRENT USERS)
================================================================================

If you are the current user setting up backups for the first time, or
verifying your backup strategy, you need to protect two things:

PROTECT YOUR SEED PHRASE:

    1. During setup, the app showed you 12 words. If you wrote them on paper,
       that paper IS your backup. But paper burns, floods, and degrades.

    2. For durable storage, stamp each word into a stainless steel plate
       using a center punch and letter stamps. Metal seed plates are sold
       by multiple vendors — search for "Bitcoin seed plate" or "metal seed
       storage." Look for stainless steel plates with a grid for stamping
       individual letters. Center punch style plates (where you mark each
       letter with a single punch strike on a grid) are the simplest and
       most durable option.

    3. Store the metal plate somewhere secure and SEPARATE from your computer:
       - A home safe (fireproof preferred)
       - A bank safe deposit box
       - With a trusted family member in a sealed envelope

    4. NEVER photograph your seed phrase.
       NEVER type it into any website.
       NEVER store it in a notes app, email, cloud drive, or any digital format.
       NEVER share it with anyone you do not fully trust with your entire vault.

PROTECT YOUR ENCRYPTED FILES:

    1. Your encrypted vault folder (default: ~/Documents/BitcoinVault/) must
       be backed up separately. The files in this folder are encrypted — they
       are safe to store in the cloud or on any backup medium.

    2. Recommended backup methods (use at least one):
       - iCloud Drive: If the vault folder is in your Documents folder and
         iCloud Drive syncs your Documents, you already have a cloud backup.
       - Time Machine: If you use Time Machine with an external drive, the
         vault folder is included automatically.
       - Manual copy: Periodically copy the vault folder to an external USB
         drive and store it separately from your computer.

    3. Verify your backup periodically. Once every few months, check that
       your backup location has the vault folder and that it contains .enc
       files with recent dates.

WHAT TO TELL YOUR INHERITOR
================================================================================

If you want someone to be able to access your vault after you die or become
incapacitated, give them:

    1. This document (printed or as a file they can access)

    2. Access to your 12-word seed phrase. Options:
       - Tell them where the metal plate is stored
       - Include it in a sealed envelope in a safe deposit box
       - Give it to an attorney with instructions

    3. Access to your encrypted vault folder. Options:
       - Share your iCloud account credentials (in your estate plan)
       - Give them the Time Machine backup drive
       - Give them a USB drive with a copy of the encrypted folder

    4. One sentence of instruction:
       "Install the Bitcoin Vault app from github.com/Evapotrack/bitcoin-vault.
       Choose Restore Existing Vault. Enter the 12 words from the metal plate.
       Point to the encrypted folder on the backup drive."

If you have enabled the dead man's switch (V2 feature): the vault will
automatically remove the Bitcoin payment requirement after your set countdown
expires (e.g., 90 days without a proof-of-life payment). Your inheritor will
only need the seed phrase and the encrypted folder — no Bitcoin wallet or
Bitcoin knowledge required. The files are still encrypted and require the
seed, but no payment is needed to unlock.


================================================================================
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: I LOST MY SEED PHRASE BUT STILL HAVE THE APP RUNNING ON MY MAC.
   CAN I GET MY SEED BACK?

A: No. The seed phrase is never shown again after initial setup. It is stored
   encrypted in your Mac's Keychain, but the app does not provide any way to
   display it. If you have lost your written backup, your options are:

   1. Create a NEW vault with a new seed phrase.
   2. Unlock your current vault, copy all files out of the vault to a regular
      folder on your Mac.
   3. Add those files to the new vault.
   4. Send any Bitcoin from the old vault's wallet to the new vault's address.
   5. Write down and properly back up the new seed phrase.

   This is your only option. There is no recovery without the seed.


Q: MY MAC WAS STOLEN. ARE MY FILES SAFE?

A: Your encrypted files are protected by AES-256-GCM encryption derived from
   your seed phrase. Without the seed, the files are computationally
   impossible to decrypt with any current or foreseeable technology.

   However, the thief has your Mac, which has the seed stored in its Keychain.
   If they can log into your Mac (they know your password, or your Mac was
   unlocked when stolen), they may be able to access the vault.

   Immediately:
   1. If you have your seed, set up a new vault on a different Mac and restore.
   2. Send any Bitcoin from the vault's wallet to a new address you control
      (before the thief does).
   3. Consider your files potentially compromised if the Mac was unlocked
      or the login password was weak.


Q: CAN I RESTORE ON A WINDOWS OR LINUX COMPUTER?

A: No. Bitcoin Vault is a macOS application. You need a Mac to restore.


Q: THE APP SAYS "COULD NOT DECRYPT" BUT I'M SURE MY SEED IS CORRECT.

A: Double-check:
   - Are all 12 words spelled correctly?
   - Are they in the exact right order?
   - Is the encrypted folder from THIS vault (not a different vault with a
     different seed)?
   - Is the encrypted folder complete (not partially copied)?

   If you are absolutely certain the seed and folder are correct, the
   encrypted vault index may be corrupted. Check for an older backup of
   the vault folder (Time Machine, iCloud version history) and try again.


Q: WHAT IF MEMPOOL.SPACE IS DOWN AND I CANNOT UNLOCK?

A: The vault requires an internet connection to verify Bitcoin payments. If
   mempool.space is temporarily down, wait and try again later. Outages are
   typically brief. The app will show an error message but will not crash or
   lose data.
